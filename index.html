Keeping your little monsters safe this season!

Three top tips for a safer Halloween

While Halloween is meant to be all about scares, there are some horrors that we know you’ll want to avoid, and we’re here to help with that. Follow our simple steps to make sure that your festivities are filled with treats, not tricks.

It's Okay To Ask For Help

Asking for help with mental health is a strong and courageous step. Just like physical health, mental well-being can have its ups and downs, and it’s completely normal to seek support when things feel overwhelming. You deserve to feel supported and understood, and there’s no shame in prioritizing your mental health. Whether it’s through therapy, support groups, or just talking to someone you trust, taking that step can lead to meaningful changes and a brighter outlook.

Take 1 Day At A Time

Life can often feel overwhelming, especially when we think too far ahead. Embracing the idea of "one day at a time" can help ground us. Each day brings its own challenges and opportunities, and focusing on the present moment allows us to manage stress more effectively. Remember, it’s okay to have tough days. Allow yourself to feel your emotions without judgment. Surround yourself with supportive people and don’t hesitate to reach out for help.

Be Kind To Yourself

Lots of sweets are unfortunately choking hazards, with round / oval boiled sweets being the worst. Check what sweeties are being handed out and siphon off those that don’t feel safe – anything that is very round / grape shaped and hard should be avoided. If your child is older and out with friends, ask them to leave any boiled sweets until they are at home – running around excitedly with friends is really not a sensible time to be consuming these. Marshmallows, especially the mini ones are definitely ones to avoid. They are roughly the same size as a 2-3 year old’s airway, and if swallowed whole can get lodged in the airway, completely obstructing breathing.
